# Definitive module map

## Platform and administration

Tenant, group company, legal entity, branch, site and warehouse hierarchy; users, teams, roles, permissions, approval limits and segregation of duties; module entitlements; currencies, tax jurisdictions, calendars and fiscal periods; workflow, approvals, notifications and escalations; document management, signatures and retention; audit, privacy and compliance; integration hub, API keys, webhooks and mappings; subscription and support administration.

## Master data management

Products, variants, packs, cases, pallets, units and conversions; raw materials, packaging and finished goods; bills of material, recipes, routings and specifications; brands, categories, allergens and certifications; customers, suppliers, distributors, retailers and contacts; sites, warehouses, bins, territories, routes and zones; price lists, tax codes and terms; stewardship, duplicate detection, approvals and history.

## Procurement and sourcing

Requisitions, RFQs and supplier comparisons; purchase orders, call-offs and blanket orders; supplier onboarding, scorecards, audits and certificates; lead times, MOQs and landed cost; inbound appointments, receipt, discrepancies and claims; import/export documentation; supplier rebates; procurement planning tied to MRP and forecasts.

## Production, quality and maintenance

Demand and forecast production plans; capacity, line, shift, labor and machine scheduling; batch/process manufacturing; work orders, issue, yield, scrap and rework; lot/serial/expiry genealogy; inspections, holds, release and non-conformance; HACCP and recall workflows; preventive maintenance, downtime, spares and OEE; co-manufacturer and subcontract production.

## Workforce management

Employee profiles, skills, contracts and certifications; shift templates and rosters; time and attendance, breaks, overtime and approvals; line/warehouse assignment; mobile clock-in under policy; leave, training and expiry alerts; payroll export and labor-cost allocation; safety incidents and corrective actions.

## Inventory and warehouse management

Multi-warehouse and multi-owner stock; receiving, put-away, replenishment, picking, packing and dispatch; barcode, QR, GS1 and labels; lot, batch, serial, expiry, FEFO/FIFO and status; aging, slow-moving, obsolete and near-expiry reporting; quarantine, samples, damage and write-offs; counts and variance approval; transfers, consignment, VMI and cross-docking; valuation, landed cost and stock ledger; cold-chain evidence.

## Sales and order management

Leads, quotations, sales orders and recurring orders; B2B, B2C, telesales, field and EDI orders; ATP, allocation and reservation; customer assortments, pricing and credit; promotions, bundles and free goods; delivery planning and proof; returns, refusals, replacements and credit notes; commissions and targets.

## Finance, accounting and invoicing

Double-entry general ledger; AR, AP and cash; invoices, notes and statements; allocation, aging, collections and dunning; tax, withholding and e-invoicing adapters; bank import, reconciliation and payment runs; fixed assets; budgets, accruals and period close; consolidation, intercompany and multi-currency; profitability by SKU, customer, channel, territory and campaign; immutable posting audit.

## CRM and customer service

Accounts, contacts and hierarchies; calls, emails, meetings, notes and contact history; opportunities, pipelines, activities and follow-ups; cases, complaints and service levels; samples, onboarding and customer success; tasks, shared inbox and templates; consent and suppression; 360-degree customer and retailer view.

## Field sales, mapping, routing and zoning

Territory, route, beat, zone and call-cycle design; map-based outlet planning; constraint-aware route optimization; daily journey plans and visit execution; check-in/out and proof-of-visit; field order capture, collections and returns; audits, planograms, availability and share of shelf; photos, signatures, questionnaires and competitor observations; offline-first execution; mileage, travel time and productivity.

## Distributor management

Distributor onboarding, contracts, territories and performance; sell-in/sell-through/sell-out; distributor stock and aging; secondary sales and outlet coverage; targets, incentives, claims and settlement; credit and overdue management; distributor portals and documents; sub-distributor structures.

## Retailer and outlet management

Chains, banners, branches, independents and buying groups; outlet classification, potential, channel and service level; assortments, listings, planograms and compliance; visit history, orders, returns, invoices and payments; store-specific prices and promotions; equipment and asset custody; delivery restrictions and geocoding.

## Commercial terms, agreements, returns and rebates

Versioned agreements and approvals; on/off-invoice discounts; retroactive volume rebates and growth incentives; listing fees, marketing funds and display allowances; free goods and promotional stock; accruals, forecasts and settlement; claims, deductions, disputes and evidence; return authorization and disposition; expiry/renewal workflow; profitability simulation before approval.

## E-commerce and marketplace

B2B ordering portal and account center; D2C storefront and headless API; catalog, content, media, search and merchandising; customer-specific catalog, price and availability; cart, checkout, payments, tax and shipping adapters; subscriptions and repeat orders; marketplace/retailer connectors; order orchestration and fulfillment routing; returns/refunds; digital shelf, reviews and content syndication; channel P&L.

## Marketing campaigns and growth

Campaign calendar, briefs, budgets and approvals; CRM/sales/geography/behavior segments; email, SMS, WhatsApp, push and in-app adapters; trade campaigns, retailer programs and distributor activations; consumer promotions, coupons, referral and loyalty; retail media and marketplace ads; assets, localization and brand governance; UTM/coupon/order/revenue attribution; A/B tests, holdouts and lift; journeys and nurture; consent, frequency caps and suppression; spend, accruals, ROI and post-campaign analysis.

## Planning, analytics and AI

Executive and role-specific dashboards; demand forecasting and consensus planning; S&OP/IBP; inventory, service level and working-capital analytics; sales, margin, price and promotion analytics; route and coverage analytics; customer/SKU profitability; anomaly detection and explainable recommendations; authorized natural-language search; auditable scenarios; export to customer BI platforms.

## Optional industry packs

Food and beverage; personal care/cosmetics; household products; OTC/pharma compliance; restricted-market products; frozen/cold chain; fresh/short shelf-life; importer/distributor-only; contract manufacturing.
